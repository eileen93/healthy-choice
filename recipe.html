GI of Sweetners

We are using natural sweetners which have lower GI than artificial sweetners.

The most common natural sweeteners are Honey, Stevia, Maple Syrup, Coconut Palm Sugar and Molasses. All of these contain calories with the exception of Stevia. Please note that I do not include Agave Syrup in the list as I do not regard it as a natural or healthy product. (Like high fructose corn syrup enzymes are used to convert the Agave starch to fructose. Natural Agave is barely sweet at all!)When choosing the best natural sweetener one needs to consider calories, effect on blood sugar, effect on dental hygiene, beneficial effects, taste, price and usefulness in cooking.

We recommend that you will check what kind of sweetner is put in whenever you buy food. Many of artificial sweetners are harmful for diabetic & prediabetic even though its GI value is low.read more...

Preserved Lemon Vinaigrette

Ingredients:
  • Lemon rind of one preserved lemon¹
  • 2 clove garlic, grated
  • 1/3 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/4 cup white balsamic vinegar
  • 1 cup Light olive oil or mild-flavored oil
  • Pinch of fresh ground white peppercorns (or black peppercorns)


Preserved lemons are lemons that have been preserved in salt and water. You can find them at gourmet and Middle Eastern markets

Instructions:
  • Remove one preserved lemon¹ from the jar
  • inse it under the cold water tap.
  • Pat dry with a paper towel.
  • Cut the lemon rind into smaller pieces and place in the blender/processor.
  • Add the grated garlic, lemon juice, honey and the white balsamic vinegar.
  • Process for 1-minute.
  • Add the oil to the processor and blend the mixture for another 30-seconds until smooth and creamy.
  • Taste and season with a pinch of white pepper.
  • Transfer the vinaigrette to a glass jar with a lid and refrigerate until needed.

Mustard-Champagne Vinaigrette

Ingredients:
  • 1/3 cup avocado oil or olive oil
  • 2/3 cup champagne vinegar
  • 1 Tablespoon chopped shallots
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp salt
Instructions:
  • Combine chopped shallots, champagne vinegar, minced garlic, Dijon mustard, honey, ground black pepper, salt into the blender. Blend for about 1 minute.
  • While blender running, slowly add the avocado oil.
  • Check seasoning, adjust if necessary.
  • Dressing stays well covered up to about 5-6 days in the fridge.

Mild Jalapeno-Lime Dressing

Ingredients:
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/4 cup light flavored olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2-4 small jalapeño peppers halved with stem and seeds removed
Instructions:
  • Place the lime juice, vinegar, honey, oil and salt in the blender.
  • Add 2 jalapeño peppers and puree until mostly smooth.
  • Taste and add more jalapeños according to however much heat you like.
  • Pour into a jar and refrigerate until needed.
  • Shake well before drizzling over your favorite salad.

Caesar Dressing

Ingredients:
  • 2 egg yolks (pasteurized, if desired)
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp anchovy paste
  • 1 tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 garlic clove pressed or minced
  • 1/2 cup light flavored olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp grated parmigiano reggiano
Instructions:
  • In a medium bowl, whisk to combine the egg yolks, lemon juice, anchovy paste, dijon mustard, garlic, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  • While whisking, add a couple drops of olive oil, then slowly drizzle in more in a steady stream. Don't add the oil too fast. Distribute the addition of the oil over 60-90 seconds of whisking.
  • The dressing should have a thick texture. Gently stir in the cheese, then place the dressing in the fridge until ready to use

Chimichurri Sauce

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups fresh parsley (no need to chop it up at all)
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, cut into large pieces
  • 1/2 of a red onion, cut into large pieces
  • 2 garlic cloves, removed from the paper and cut in half
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup white balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
Instructions:
  • Place all ingredients in a food processor.
  • Process on high for about 30 seconds, scraping down the sides of the bowl about halfway through.
  • Pulse until sauce has a uniform, medium-fine chunky consistency like you see in the photos.
  • Serve over proteins like steak or salmon, vegetables or whatever suits your fancy!

Moroccan Chicken Braise

Ingredients:
  • 9 chicken thighs, bone in, skin on, patted dry
  • 1 large sweet onion, finely diced
  • 3 - 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup canned black olives, chopped, and draine
  • 3/4 tsp. Turmeric
  • 1 tsp. Paprika
  • 2 tsp. Ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp. Cayenne
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp. Fresh ginger, minced
  • 4 tsp. Honey
  • 1 tsp. Salt
  • 1 preserved lemons
  • 2 winter squashes or carrots, organic, sliced
  • 1 Tbsp. flour, can be gluten free
  • 1 Tbsp. chicken broth
  • 3&1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tsp. Avocado oil
Instructions:
click for letter recipe
  • Be sure to pat your chicken dry and then season it with salt.
  • Preheat a large stainless steel frying pan over medium high heat.
  • Add the oil and swirl it around the pan.
  • Add the chicken skin side down to the pan.
  • Let the chicken sear in the pan on the first side for about 4 minutes.
  • Turn the pieces over with tongs and cook on the second side for another 3 minutes.
  • The exterior of the chicken should be a golden brown.
  • Remove the pieces to a plate and keep warm.
  • Turn the heat on the pan to medium low and add the diced onion.
  • Saute this for a good 30 to 45 minutes stirring frequently.
  • After a good 35 minutes, add the minced garlic and saute for an additional 2 minutes stirring regularly.
  • Pour in the 3 ½ cups of chicken broth to deglaze the pan.
  • Stir that fond off the bottom of the pan so it can flavor your sauce.
  • pour in all the spices including the cinnamon stick, paprika, cumin, and turmeric
  • Add the ginger, carrots, the preserved lemon wedges, and the salt and stir to combine.
  • Increase the temperature to bring this to a boil and then reduce it so it simmers.
  • In a small bowl, combine the flour and 1 Tbsp. of chicken broth.
  • Slowly pour this into the sauce while stirring to avoid lumps.
  • Add the honey and stir to dissolve it.
  • Place the chicken thighs back into the sauce, cover and simmer for 30 - 45 minutes
  • Remove the cinnamon stick and the lemon wedges.
  • Pull out the chicken thighs onto a cutting board and let them cool for 5 minutes.
  • Remove the skin and then take the meat off the bone leaving the pieces in good size chunks.
  • Place the chicken meat back into the pot and place it back on the stove to ensure everything is hot.
  • Once hot pull the pan off the stove and add the olives and stir to combine.
